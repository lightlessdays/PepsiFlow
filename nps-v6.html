What is NPS?

A government-backed retirement savings scheme regulated by the Pension Fund Regulatory and Development Authority (PFRDA) under the Ministry of Finance.

The National Pension System (NPS) is a voluntary, long-term retirement savings scheme open to all Indian citizens between the ages of 18 and 70. Contributions are invested across asset classes — equity, corporate bonds, and government securities — generating market-linked returns over the long term.

At retirement (age 60), subscribers may withdraw up to 60% of the accumulated corpus as a tax-free lump sum under Section 10(12A). The remaining 40% is mandatorily used to purchase a lifelong annuity from a PFRDA-empanelled Annuity Service Provider. The annuity income received thereafter is taxable as per the applicable income tax slab.

Tax Benefits

NPS & Tax Efficiency

Retirement savings that reduce your tax bill today, and pay you for life tomorrow. Key provisions under the Income Tax Act, 1961:

  • Employee contributions to Tier I are deductible up to 10% of salary (Basic + DA) under Section 80CCD(1), within the overall ₹1.5 lakh limit under Section 80CCE.
  • An additional deduction of up to ₹50,000 is available under Section 80CCD(1B), over and above the ₹1.5 lakh ceiling.
  • Employer contributions are deductible under Section 80CCD(2) — up to 10% of salary under the old tax regime and up to 14% under the new tax regime.
  • At retirement, lump sum withdrawal of up to 60% of the corpus is fully exempt under Section 10(12A).
  • The amount used to purchase an annuity is exempt at the point of purchase under Section 80CCD(5). Annuity income received thereafter is taxable as per your applicable slab.

For complete details, refer to the official NPS Trust website: npstrust.org.in

FAQs

Questions that matter

Tier I is locked until age 60. Partial withdrawal of up to 25% of your own contributions is permitted after 3 years for specific purposes such as higher education, medical emergency, or home purchase. Exit before 60 requires a minimum of 80% of the corpus to be used for purchasing an annuity.
Your PRAN (Permanent Retirement Account Number) belongs to you, not your employer. It is fully portable across jobs and locations. Your new employer can continue contributing to the same account without any interruption.
At age 60, the lump sum withdrawal of up to 60% of the corpus is fully tax-free under Section 10(12A). The amount used to purchase an annuity is exempt at the point of purchase under Section 80CCD(5). However, the monthly pension received from the annuity is taxable as income in the year of receipt, as per your applicable slab.
Check with your HR or Compensation team about the NPS enrolment window — most employers open this during their annual or mid-year compensation review cycle. Review your employer's flexible compensation or benefits policy and make the necessary selection on the designated portal. Share your PRAN with your company's Payroll team once done. No PRAN yet? Contact your HR team and they will connect you with the appropriate vendor to generate one.
